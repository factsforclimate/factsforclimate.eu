Don't be told what to eat, drink or wear, but it's good to hear the two sides of the story about livestock. Because when you make a choice, you also choose all of the consequences!

This is a parody of the campain "meatthefacts" started by the European livestock and fur lobby called "European Livestock Voice". This campaign aims to change the public opinion of the meat and dairy industry by ridiculing a vegeterian and vegan lifestyle with visual ad campaign in Brussels backed up with a website. The livestock sector feels victimized by interest groups "who only want to spread myths and radical views about livestock farming"(sic). The industry tries to project theirself as the underdog that has to compete against the big vegan lobby, the reality is the opposite ofcourse. The campaign site tries to influence the public opinion about the livestock industry by giving facts and by dispelling myths. It mainly dispells statements often found in commentairy sections of social media posts. While it is true that practices, facts and figures from outside the EU that are often found on social media aren't always exactly the same as those of the EU, it is still true that billions of animals die and suffer because of the livestock industry. The stakeholders of this campain include the European Livestock and Meat Trades Union that represents the Debra group (mishandeling in tielt), Fur Europe
